**Slide 1 — Title**
Set the frame: this deck covers how a Knowledge Graph enriches Customer 360, the use cases it unlocks, and how we'd roll it out. Not just Identity Resolution anymore — a broader platform play.

**Slide 2 — Core Questions**
Anchor the whole deck here. Every slide that follows answers one or more of these five questions. Useful to return to this slide later if people ask "why does this matter?"

**Slide 3 — KG Enrichment**
Explain the mechanic: we don't rebuild the graph per use case. Two core tables (individual + commercial card members) are the foundation, and each additional data source we connect — transactions, relationships, life events, risk signals, interactions — unlocks new questions we can answer. This is the "one investment, many payoffs" idea, previewed here and expanded on slide 8.

**Slide 4 — Use Cases**
Two categories: Data Enrichment (makes the graph smarter) and Customer Engagement (uses that intelligence to act). Frame it as a pipeline — enrichment feeds engagement.

**Slide 5 — Regulatory & Risk**
The third lens on the same graph — oversight and defensibility rather than growth or engagement. Emphasize "same graph, different question": is this connection a risk, and can we explain why we flagged it?

**Slide 6 — Deep Dive: Preferred Time to Call**
Make one use case concrete end-to-end. Walk left to right: the business problem, what data feeds it, what comes out, and why it matters. This is proof the enrichment pattern from slide 3 actually works in practice.

**Slide 7 — Supporting Identity Resolution**
Reassure the room that Identity Resolution — the original ask — isn't lost, it's now one of the graph's supported use cases rather than the sole focus. Same relationship data, applied to "is this a duplicate or a new customer."

**Slide 8 — Architecture**
The technical payoff of slide 3's promise: build the graph once, and it serves risk, engagement, fraud, and compliance simultaneously. Good slide to address "won't this be expensive to maintain?" — no, it's one build, many consumers.

**Slide 9 — Value + Roadmap**
Close with why (top) and how (bottom). End on the roadmap so the next conversation is naturally "when do we start Phase 1?"
